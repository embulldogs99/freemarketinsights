EPS and Peter Lynch

In his book One Up on Wall Street Peter Lynch walks us through his method of investing. He suggests you should know your prospective investment company. You should buy small companies which inherently have big potential. Never buy hot stocks because they always fizzle out quickly, and find a niche company with a strong balance sheet.

However, his primary arguement is buy a stock with growing dividends and EPS.

We see Walmart experienced strong stock price growth as dividends and earnings skyrocket

Another example is Ford
This is not proof that dividends and earnings Cause stock price growth.
We are simply observing a strong correlation with explanatory power.



Peter Lynch obviously knows what he is doing and has sold over a million copies of his book: implying other traders are investing this way also; which strengthens this trend.

This interests us, NOT to go and find a random small stock that has increasing Dividends and EPS and invest in it. Here at Insights, we search for anomolies. We look at the market, determine how it functions, and find instances when it temporarily breaks; knowing that the market will fix itself with time and we will be the ones who got in early and profit

So we look at the data

Below we show Basic EPS for the SnP 600 Small
We observe if the EPS today was larger than the prior EPS
If so, we reward a 1.
We filter and are left with the stocks who have grown EPS most successfully over the past 9 reporting periods.

The Average stock price of the SnP 600 is $38
Interestingly, The Average stock price of these top 50 stocks is $52: Evidence that the market trades on EPS growth

Highlighted in yellow, we find a couple outlier stocks, much cheaper than the rest.
We can see that MDXG recently did not grow EPS, and has been severly punished for it
Missing EPS may be an oportune buying catalyst for the intelligent investor
We are left with ORIT as our anomoly worth further investigation.
